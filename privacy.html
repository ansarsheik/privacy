Privacy Policy

Last updated 29 May 2026 · Effective 29 May 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Areeya Social Publisher (“the App”, “we”, “our”, “us”) handles information when the operator uses the App to publish their own short videos to connected social media platforms (Instagram, Facebook, YouTube, and TikTok).

The App is a single-operator personal automation tool. It is operated by one individual (the “Operator”) on their own computer. It does not provide a service to the public, does not host user accounts, and does not collect data from anyone other than the Operator.

1. Who this policy applies to

  • The Operator — the single individual who installs, configures, and uses the App on their personal device.
  • People who appear in videos the Operator publishes through the App, to the extent any personal data of theirs is contained in the video file.

The App does not interact with viewers of the published content. Viewers' data is handled solely by the destination platforms (TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, YouTube) under their own privacy policies.

2. Information the App handles

2.1 Provided by the Operator

  • Video files sent by the Operator to a private Telegram bot.
  • Topic prompts and captions the Operator types into the Telegram bot.
  • API access tokens the Operator obtains from TikTok, Meta (Instagram/Facebook), and Google (YouTube) and stores in a local configuration file.

2.2 Generated by the App

  • AI-generated text (titles, captions, hashtags, on-screen overlay text) produced by the OpenAI API from the video and topic.
  • Processed videos (vertical 9:16 renders with text overlay) produced locally by FFmpeg.
  • Operational logs (job state, timestamps, target platform, errors) stored in a local SQLite database on the Operator's computer.

2.3 Received from platform APIs

When the Operator authorizes the App, the platform issues an access token, an internal user identifier (e.g. TikTok open_id, Instagram Business Account ID, YouTube channel ID), and basic profile fields (display name, avatar URL). This information is stored locally and used solely to publish the Operator's own content back to the same authenticated account.

3. How TikTok data is used (scope-by-scope)

The App requests the following TikTok Login Kit / Content Posting API scopes. Each scope is used only for the purpose listed.

  • user.info.basic — Retrieves the Operator's open_id, union_id, display name, and avatar URL so the App can confirm which TikTok account is connected and show that account in the Telegram preview. This information is stored locally on the Operator's computer and is never shared with third parties.
  • video.upload — Uploads the Operator's own processed video file to the Operator's TikTok account as a draft, so the Operator can finalize and post it from within the TikTok app. The video sent is only the file the Operator submitted to the App; no other content is accessed.
  • video.publish — Directly publishes the Operator's own processed video, along with the AI-generated title and hashtags approved by the Operator in Telegram, to the Operator's TikTok account. Used only when the Operator taps the “Publish to TikTok” button.

The App does not request, read, or store any other TikTok data, including but not limited to: other users' videos, comments, followers, direct messages, search results, or analytics about anyone other than the Operator's own content actions performed by the App.

4. How Meta (Instagram & Facebook) data is used

  • Instagram Business publishing — Uploads the Operator's processed video and caption to the Operator's own Instagram Business account using the Instagram Graph API.
  • Facebook Page publishing — Posts the Operator's processed video and caption to a Facebook Page the Operator manages.
  • Stored locally: the Page/Instagram Business ID, long-lived access token, and the Operator's display name. No other Meta data is accessed.

5. How Google (YouTube) data is used

The App uses the YouTube Data API v3 with the youtube.upload scope to upload the Operator's processed video to the Operator's own YouTube channel as a Short. No other YouTube data is read or stored.

6. How OpenAI is used

The App sends the Operator-provided topic and sampled frames or transcripts from the Operator-provided video to the OpenAI API to generate captions, titles, hashtags, and overlay text. Per OpenAI's API data policy, API data is not used to train OpenAI models.

7. Where data is stored

  • On the Operator's computer — videos, processed renders, AI-generated text, access tokens, and the SQLite job database. The computer is protected by full-disk encryption and an account password.
  • On a single private virtual machine controlled by the Operator — processed videos are uploaded over SFTP to obtain a public URL required by the Instagram Graph API. Files are removed when the related job is no longer needed.
  • On platform servers — once published, content is hosted by TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, or YouTube under their own terms.

No data is stored on any cloud server operated by us other than the Operator's own VM and the destination platforms.

8. Sharing of data

The App does not sell, rent, or share any data with third parties for marketing or advertising. Data is transmitted only to:

  • The destination platform the Operator chose to publish to (TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, YouTube), via that platform's official API.
  • OpenAI, to generate captions and overlay text.
  • Telegram, which transports the messages between the Operator and the private bot.

9. Data retention

  • Raw video files received via the bot — kept up to 30 days on the Operator's computer for retry/debugging, then deleted.
  • Processed videos — kept for record-keeping after publication.
  • Operational logs — kept indefinitely; contain no third-party personal data.
  • Access tokens — kept while valid; expired or revoked tokens are deleted.
  • The Operator may delete any of the above at any time by removing the local files.

10. Your rights

If your personal data has ended up in the App (for example, you appear in a video the Operator processed), you may email the address below to request:

  • Confirmation of whether your data is held;
  • Deletion of the related video and derived files;
  • Correction of inaccurate information;
  • A copy of the information held.

Where UK GDPR applies, you may also lodge a complaint with the UK Information Commissioner's Office.

11. Revoking platform access

  • TikTok — Settings → Privacy → Authorized apps, then remove “Areeya Social Publisher”.
  • Instagram / Facebook — Settings → Apps and Websites, then remove the connected app.
  • Google / YouTube — Third-party apps with account access, then remove the App.

12. Children

The App is not intended for or directed at children under 13 (or under 16 where local law requires a higher age). No data about children is knowingly processed.

13. Security

All network traffic to platform APIs uses HTTPS / TLS. Access tokens are stored locally in protected files. The Operator's computer is encrypted at rest. No production database is exposed to the public internet.

14. Compliance with platform terms

Use of TikTok data complies with the TikTok Developer Terms of Service. Use of Meta data complies with the Meta Platform Terms and Developer Policies. Use of YouTube data complies with the YouTube API Services Terms and the Google Privacy Policy.

If material changes are made, the “Last updated” date above will change. The previous version remains in the page's git history at the public repository.
